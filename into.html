Introduction to E-Waste

E-waste, or electronic waste, refers to discarded electronic devices. With rapid technological advancements, old gadgets become obsolete quickly, leading to a surge in e-waste. Proper recycling and disposal of e-waste help prevent environmental pollution and promote resource recovery.

Many electronic components contain hazardous substances like lead, mercury, and cadmium, which can pollute the environment if not handled properly. Recycling e-waste reduces landfill waste and allows the recovery of valuable materials such as gold, silver, and copper.

Improper disposal of e-waste contributes to environmental degradation, harming soil, water, and air quality. Raising awareness and implementing sustainable recycling practices can mitigate these risks, ensuring a cleaner and healthier future for generations to come.
